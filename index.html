Overview of Hydrology & Hydraulics

What is Hydrology?

Hydrology is the scientific study of water's properties, distribution, and effects on Earth's surface, soils, and atmosphere.

Hydrological Cycle Components

Key processes include precipitation, infiltration, runoff, interception, evaporation, and evapotranspiration, among others.

Hydrographs

A hydrograph plots the rate of flow versus time from a stream at a specific point. Factors such as peak discharge and base flow are critical for analysis.

Stormwater Management

Management techniques focus on mitigating flood risks and managing runoff, especially in urban settings.

Impact of Land Development

Development increases surface runoff and alters hydrological pathways, increasing flood risks and necessitating robust stormwater systems.
