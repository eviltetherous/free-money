Hydrology & Hydraulics Overview

What is Hydrology?

Hydrology is the scientific study of water’s properties, distribution, and effects on the earth’s surface, in the soil, and in the atmosphere.

Hydrological Cycle

The cycle includes Water Vapour in clouds, Precipitation, Infiltration, Sea, Lakes, Rivers, Surface Runoff, Interception, Evaporation, and Evapotranspiration.

Where does the water go?

  • Interception - Precipitation is abstracted by vegetation or small surface depressions.
  • Infiltration - Precipitation seeps through the earth’s surface and percolates downward.
  • Evapotranspiration - Water absorbed by vegetation and then vaporized back to the atmosphere.
  • Surface Runoff - Water flows over the ground’s surface to sea, rivers, and streams when precipitation rate exceeds infiltration and evapotranspiration rates.
  • Evaporation - Takes place at sea/water surfaces, replenishing water vapour in the atmosphere.

Rainfall and Measurement

Rainfall volume is calculated as Rainfall depth x area. Rainfall intensity affects hydrological factors such as infiltration and runoff rates.

Hydrographs

Hydrographs plot the rate of flow (discharge) versus time from a stream at a specific point, useful for analyzing the effects of rainfall over a period.

Stormwater Management

Stormwater control facilities are essential to mitigate flooding and water pollution, requiring careful design to handle surface runoff effectively.

Drainage Design & Layout

Design considerations include the network of inlets and pipes designed for gravity flow towards streams or reservoirs, essential for urban infrastructure.

Development Impact on Land

Urban development increases surface runoff and decreases infiltration, necessitating robust drainage systems to manage increased hydrological load.
